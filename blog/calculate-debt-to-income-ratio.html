How to calculate your debt-to-income ratio

Lenders calculate your DTI before approving any loan. Here's how to calculate it yourself in 3 steps — so there are no surprises when you apply.

CheckAfford
May 2025
6 min read

Your debt-to-income ratio (DTI) is the number lenders trust more than almost anything else. The calculation takes 5 minutes and can save you months of wasted time on applications you won't be approved for.

Step 1: Add Up All Monthly Debt Payments

Debt TypeExampleInclude?
Minimum credit card payments$85✓ Yes
Car loan payment$420✓ Yes
Student loan payment$280✓ Yes
Mortgage/rent$1,200✓ Yes
Groceries / utilities—✕ No
Insurance / subscriptions—✕ No

Step 2: Find Your Gross Monthly Income

  • Salary: Annual salary ÷ 12 ($72,000/year = $6,000/month)
  • Hourly: Rate × 40 hours × 52 weeks ÷ 12
  • Self-employed: Average net income over last 2 years ÷ 12

Step 3: Divide and Multiply

The Calculation

Total monthly debt: $2,135
Gross monthly income: $6,000

$2,135 ÷ $6,000 = 0.356
0.356 × 100 = 35.6% DTI

Result: At the acceptable edge. Most lenders will approve, but best rates require under 36%.
