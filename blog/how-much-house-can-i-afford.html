How much house can I afford on my salary?

Banks will approve you for more than you should borrow. Here's how to calculate the number that actually works for your life.

CheckAfford
May 2025
9 min read

Most people walk into a realtor's office without knowing their real budget — they just wait to see how much the bank approves. Loan approval and true affordability are two very different things.

The 28/36 Rule: The Foundation

The 28/36 Rule

28% — max % of gross income for housing costs (mortgage + taxes + insurance)
36% — max % of gross income for ALL debt combined

These numbers assume no other significant debt and a 20% down payment. If you have car payments or student loans, your real affordable price is lower.

What "Housing Costs" Actually Includes

The 28% rule applies to your total housing payment — not just mortgage principal and interest. Include: property taxes (1–2%/year), homeowner's insurance ($100–200/mo), PMI if under 20% down, and HOA fees if applicable.

How Existing Debt Changes Everything

Real Example — $80,000 Salary

Without other debts: Can afford ~$285,000 home
With $600/mo car payment: Can afford ~$210,000 home
With $600 car + $400 student loans: Can afford ~$155,000 home
Same salary. Very different home budgets.
