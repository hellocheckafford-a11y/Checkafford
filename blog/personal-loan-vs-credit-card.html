Personal loan vs credit card: which is better?

Both can help you manage debt — but they work very differently. Here's an honest comparison of when a personal loan beats a credit card, and when it doesn't.

CheckAfford
May 2025
7 min read

If you're carrying credit card debt at 20–25% APR, a personal loan at 10–15% APR can save you thousands. But it's not always that simple.

The Core Difference

FactorPersonal LoanCredit Card
Interest rate6–36% APR (fixed)18–29% APR (variable)
PaymentFixed monthly paymentFlexible (minimum or more)
Term12–84 months (set)Open-ended (revolving)
Best forLarge amounts, fixed timelineSmall amounts, flexibility needed

When a Personal Loan Is Better

  • You have $5,000+ in high-interest credit card debt
  • Your credit score is 680+ — you'll qualify for rates that beat your card APR
  • You want a fixed payoff date and structured payments
  • You trust yourself not to run the cards back up
Real Savings Example

$8,000 credit card debt at 22% APR, $250/month → 4+ years, ~$4,000 in interest
Personal loan at 11% APR, $250/month → 3.5 years, ~$1,800 in interest
Savings: ~$2,200 — just by switching the vehicle.

The Danger Nobody Talks About

The most common mistake: take out a personal loan to pay off credit cards, then gradually run the cards back up. Now you have both. If you consolidate, freeze or close the paid-off cards.
