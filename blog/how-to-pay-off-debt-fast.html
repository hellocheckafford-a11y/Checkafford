How to pay off $10,000 in debt fast

Ten thousand dollars in debt feels overwhelming. But with the right method and a clear monthly target, most people can eliminate it in 12–24 months.

CheckAfford
May 2025
8 min read

$10,000 in debt is one of the most common financial situations in America. Whether it's credit cards, a personal loan, or a mix — the path out is the same. What changes is how fast you travel it.

Step 1: Know Exactly What You Owe

Your Debt Inventory

Write down every debt: Creditor | Balance | Interest Rate (APR) | Minimum Payment

Example: Chase Visa $3,200 at 24.99% · Discover $4,100 at 19.99% · Personal Loan $2,700 at 11.5%
Total: $10,000 | Min payments: ~$232/mo

Step 2: Choose Your Method

Avalanche Method — pay minimums on everything, put extra toward the highest interest rate first. Saves the most money. Best for disciplined people motivated by math.

Snowball Method — pay minimums on everything, put extra toward the smallest balance first. Fastest psychological wins. Research shows higher completion rates.
